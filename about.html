About Political Data Hub

No spin. No bias. Just official data.

Our Mission

American political discourse has devolved into a competition of fear. Democrats warn about authoritarian takeovers, Republicans warn about socialist dystopiasand both parties quietly expand federal power while you're distracted by culture war theater.

Political Data Hub exists to cut through this noise. We compile official government data, academic research, and verified statistics to show what's actually happening in American politicsnot what campaign ads and partisan pundits want you to believe.

We don't take positions. We don't endorse candidates. We don't accept funding from political organizations. We just present the numbers and let you draw your own conclusions.

Our Methodology

Every data point on this site comes from official, publicly accessible sources. We prioritize:

  • Government Agencies: Office of Management and Budget (OMB), Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED), Census Bureau, Congressional Budget Office (CBO), Government Accountability Office (GAO)
  • Legislative Records: Federal Register, Code of Federal Regulations, Congressional Research Service reports
  • Independent Research: National Bureau of Economic Research (NBER), Mercatus Center regulatory data, peer-reviewed academic papers
  • Transparency Organizations: OpenSecrets (campaign finance), USA Spending (federal contracts)

All charts are normalized to a base year (typically 1975 = 1.0) to show growth trends independent of inflation or population changes. This allows direct comparison across metrics with different scales.

What We're NOT

We are not a political campaign, advocacy organization, or opinion platform. We don't tell you how to vote, which policies to support, or what conclusions to draw.

We are not funded by political parties, PACs, corporations, or partisan think tanks. This site is maintained independently.

We are not claiming neutrality is easy or that data tells the whole storybut we believe starting with accurate facts is better than starting with partisan talking points.
